Language and Culture in Montréal

The official language in Montréal is French. The French language and culture was passed into Montréal by French colonizers looking for trade and economic gain. The people of Montréal have retained this culture for centuries, thus, French has become a major form of communication in Canada, particularly Quebec, the province in which Montréal is located. Here are some basic French phrases that you can use to get around the area!

Important French Phrases to Know

Bonjour = Hello

Ça va? = How are you?

Je suis... = I am...

Je m'appelle... = My name is (I am called)...

Où est... = Where is...

Comment... = How...

Merci = Thank you

Je voudrais... = I would like...

Qu'est-ce que c'est... = What is...

Culture

  • Montréal is home to the world-renowned circus, Cirque du Soleil.
  • The Montréal Canadiens is one of the most decorated ice hockey teams in the world. Also called the "Habs", they are the longest standing professional ice hockey team in the world and help contribute to Canada's love for the sport.
  • Montréal is known as a "city of festivals". It hosts over 100 festivals per year, with the primetime for festivals being in the summer. These festivals are usually hosted at the Place des Festivals.
  • Underneath Montréal is an underground city, a network of offices, restaurants, shopping malls, apartment complexes, etc., connected with a tunnel system.
